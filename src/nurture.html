Work at nurture farm

Role: Associate product designer

5 months

Designing for 80k farmers to increase buying conversation rate and helping provide better ordering experience

nurture farm has a commerce platform(app) called nurture.retail that allows Agri-input retailers and dealers to buy pesticides (insecticides, herbicides, fungicides), fertilizers, agriculture tools, and other crop protection products directly from the manufacturers at best prices.

It is India's fastest-growing farm commerce app bringing in a new age Agri input marketplace to 80,000 retailers by providing a complete range of Agritech solutions to farmers through mobile.

I joined the design team as an Associate Product Designer in July 2022. My work involves mostly improving the engagement, retention, and cart experience of the app.

We received a data which shows large number of people has queries on how to fill general info, basic info. We had a prioritized internally and decided to solve for product availability and how to use the app since these are highly impactful and which will also bring high retention rate to the business

Goals

  • Solving the issues with product availability (users not finding right products)
  • Helping people educate “how to use the app”

Helping people find right products

Forget people whether ordering or not, most of them are not even able to find the right products they wanted that are on the app.

Common Queries majorly include:

  • ....Is product X listed on nurture retail app?
  • ....Where do I find latest products in the app?

In order of to solve these problems, PM had a outline idea of how to solve the problems

Objectives:

  • Improve search to increase discovery of products
  • Improve discovery of new products

How can we increase the discovery of products

Our PM showed a data which indicated that almost 30% of the users use “Search” everyday to find the products they want. We looked at the data how the search is performing, we found that out of 30% of these searches 20% are Null Searches (doesnt return any results).

The number of queries on search we receive are high because the system triggers a search at every character (show results as you type). We want to reduce the null searches and the total number of search queries that the system handles.

More than 85% of queries can be resolved by adding, removing, or replacing just 1 character from the search results.

To solve this, the PM suggested in implementing “Elastic search” that will showing suggestions replacing, adding or removing to 1 or 2 characters in the typed phrase, to show corresponding results.

To integrate the elastic search with search, first I wanted to study & understand other apps search functionality works on successful apps. I decided to stick with only ecommerce and food apps because that’s where our app falls in. Ex: Jiomart, Blinkit, Zepto, Flipkart, Amazon

First defined the logic on how the flow should work. In order to show right suggestions and reduce the number of loading time, we decided to show the suggestions list like how google search does instead of triggering search while you’re typing.

We also had data that users search try searching through techical details of the products such pesticides, fertilizers etc(Ex: ammonia, chrolide). Similar to how we search “18GB RAM mobile” in flipkart or amazon. The search data is high so we wanted to find a way to help users search through technical details.

I tried different ways to present the results and in my attempts, I found that each iteration resulted in a more cluttered and visually overwhelming. The more the information is added, the more it gets complicated. Finally I ended up with the separating them two in separate heading with relevant searches below each.

At the end, we finally landed up on something that was more familiar and brings much easier to understand with less learning curve.

Objective 2: How to effectively educate users on how to use the app

We have received x of queries stating that people don’t know how to place an order and struggling understanding the app. Queries majorly include: How to place an order and Want to know more about the retail app.

We thought of providing Improving the in-app guidance:

  • Better visibility to the tutorial video on app (Also include link as part of the welcome message)
  • Coach marks on the app

It was expected problem as usually Farmers are not tech savvy and doesn’t have much digital literacy One of ways to educate people that we thought is through providing informational and helpful tutorial videos. We already had tutorial section in the app. But it was not very well designed and received poor engagement from the users.

Problems with current design

  • Lack of structured content to discovery tutorial videos
  • Unable to see newly launched updated videos or features

We iterated through layouts and structure with the goal of achieving best way of presenting information. Some of the designs looked best in initially, but seems not feasible if the videos are large and scaled.

Finally we settled up on the above design which seemed not taking up much space but also has enough information for the user to find videos.

Coach marks as first touchpoint

As part of the educating the users, we also included coachmarks when the user enters the new flow, discovers new features and make it easy to use the app.

Conclusion

This was my first time working with product managers and it was a new learning collaborating with them. Thanks to Ashutosh and Prashant singh for providing right feedbacks. This is still under development we’ll get to know the conversion once this goes live.
