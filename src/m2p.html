Work at M2P Fintech

Role: Research, Visual design, User testing

Team project

An end-to-end visual redesign of a forex portal with goal of increasing customer satisfaction, engagement, effectivenss and loyality.

M2P forex management platform

M2P has its platform called Forex portal which is a tool that enables banks & corporates to issue prepaid forex cards to their consumers for expenses in local currency when they travel abroad.

M2P provides all the necessary infrastructure to onboard, manage the program and issue Forex cards to the users on behalf of the banks/corporates.

The portal was designed back in 2015 and it wasn’t updated then. The business team regularly received plenty of queries, new changes, and complaints from the banks.

M2P’s user base and global scale expand from day to day. So there was an immediate need to ensure provide a seamless experience for the banks who use the tool that can hold up with that rapid growth.

This also helps keep the loyalty of the existing users and attract new users from the market.

Defining the problem

To understand the problem space, we conducted user interviews with 10 participants from the banks such as SBI, ICICI, Axis, etc. Due to the time crunch, we limited the users to 10. The business team helped us to connect with the right user for user interviews with banks and agencies. Each session lasted for 45-60 minutes.

We gained a lot of information from the interviews that allowed us to comprehend the problem area and proceed. I sat down with the team, evaluated the user interview findings, and then ranked them in order of the importance of the issue.

Problems identified:

  • Users have a difficult time interacting with the current portal. This is in terms of information hierarchy, navigation and visual design
  • Current tool lacks glancable information in the interface.
  • Accessibility and usability issues
  • Lots of unused features wasting space
  • Accessbiity and usuabliity issues
  • Lots of manual tasks which can be automated
  • Lots of confusion and uncertainty while filling forms

Validating through card sorting

We wanted to test & evaluate how users understand the current information architecture. For that, we choose to do open card sorting session with 3 bank users.

The outcome was participants grouped the cards differently compared to the existing flows and a few of them were confused about the position or failed to label the cards. So through this we were able to find out that the present design was different from how the users expected to see the set of information.

Using the frequency vs value matrix I prioritized the flows and shown the important tasks in the main menu and moved sub-tasks to the relevant categories. As a result, left menu's navigation became simpler and required less clicks. After receiving some input and making a few modifications, the team approved the revised flow.

This was particularly challenging and tricky since disrupting a pattern that individuals have grown accustomed to could frustrate them. Most people dislike change. Therefore, it was crucial for us to develop adjustments that don't take a long time to settle down and minimise the learning curves as much as possible.

Making long form filling process effortless

We have observed lot of issues with the current form that made form filling process more tedious for the users. We dissected the problems one by one and gave a visual overhaul to the entire form to make the form filling process less intimitading and effortless

How we did?

Filling Long forms is always intimating and they look like they require a lot of effort to complete (and they do). We wanted to try simplifying forms somewhat by cutting unimportant fields, but most of them are essential and unavoidable.

One of the obvious way to make the long process short is to make them fill the form by using categories with the stepper. But that idea couldn’t work here since the users wanted to quickly glance the information, makes changes & verify refering one data with other.

One of the way we decided to go is regrouping the form and removing any reduntant fields and automate the tasks if feasible.

We regrouped the entire form sections, removed reduntant fields(if any), gave appropiate lables for easy understanding. Despite the fact that we ended up adding more sections, it was simple to scan and comprehend.

Giving well-designed table experience to users

The daily tasks of users often involve working with tabular data. Users found it challenging to work with tables due to a lack of flexibility, problems with broken alignment, a lack of consistency, and incorrect information hierarchies. Therefore to solve, we researched some of the best practises for table design, gathered patterns, and revised the table.

By far the biggest challenge was to ensure that table appeared visually simple, even when dense with information and content.
